# Code of Conduct

This project is an engineering and governance-oriented runtime. We expect professional conduct in all interactions.

## 1) Purpose

We are committed to providing a harassment-free, inclusive environment for everyone who participates in this repository and its community spaces, regardless of background or identity.

## 2) Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including (but not limited to):

- GitHub issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions
- project documentation and wiki content
- CI/CD discussions and operational threads
- any public space where a person is representing the project (official email, official social account, or acting as a designated representative)

## 3) Expected Behavior

We value:

- professionalism, empathy, and respectful communication
- constructive feedback and rigorous technical disagreement without personal attacks
- accountability: own mistakes, correct them, and learn
- focus on improving the project and helping others succeed

## 4) Unacceptable Behavior

Not allowed:

- harassment, intimidation, threats, or targeted hostility
- derogatory, discriminatory, or demeaning language
- sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention
- trolling or sustained disruption of technical threads
- publishing private information (doxxing), including emails or addresses, without explicit permission
- retaliation against someone who reports a concern in good faith

## 5) Reporting and Handling

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

- For Code of Conduct violations: open a GitHub issue and apply the `conduct` label.
- For security-sensitive matters: follow `SECURITY.md` (do not disclose secrets publicly).

Reports should include:

- what happened (links/screenshots if possible)
- where it happened
- when it happened
- any relevant context (participants, prior warnings, etc.)

## 6) Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for interpreting and enforcing this Code of Conduct. They may remove, edit, or reject content (comments, commits, PRs, issues) that violates it.

Enforcement actions aim to be consistent and proportional to the behavior and its impact. Maintainers will communicate decisions and rationale when appropriate.

### Enforcement Levels

1. **Correction**
   - Impact: unprofessional or inappropriate behavior (single incident)
   - Action: request to stop; may include a private warning and clarification

2. **Warning**
   - Impact: repeated issues or a significant single incident
   - Action: formal warning; may include temporary restrictions on interaction

3. **Temporary Ban**
   - Impact: serious or sustained violations
   - Action: time-limited ban from participation or interaction

4. **Permanent Ban**
   - Impact: severe harassment, threats, doxxing, or repeated serious violations
   - Action: permanent removal from participation and community spaces
